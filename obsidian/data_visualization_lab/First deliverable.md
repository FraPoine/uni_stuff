# Data Visualization Proposal
### Western vs Eastern Animation

# 1. Topic

This project explores the differences between **Western and Eastern animated films**, focusing on how these two animation industries have evolved over time and how they differ in terms of production, popularity, and commercial success.

Western animation, mainly driven by studios such as Disney and Pixar, and Eastern animation, particularly Japanese anime, represent two of the most influential traditions in global animation. While both aim to entertain audiences through animated storytelling, they differ in cultural background, production styles, audience targets, and distribution strategies.

The project aims to compare these two animation ecosystems through a data-driven perspective.
# 2. Research Question

**How have Western and Eastern animated films evolved over time in terms of production, popularity, and global impact?**

This question allows us to investigate several dimensions of the animation industry, including production trends, audience reception, and commercial performance.
# 3. Motivation

Animation has become a major component of the global film industry and an important cultural medium worldwide. Over the past decades, animated films have expanded far beyond children’s entertainment and now attract audiences of all ages.

Western animation and Eastern animation have followed different historical trajectories. Western animation has historically dominated global box office markets, while Eastern animation—especially Japanese anime—has experienced significant international growth and cultural influence.

Understanding how these two industries have evolved and how they differ in terms of popularity, production trends, and economic success can reveal interesting insights about cultural diffusion, audience preferences, and the globalization of media.

Data visualization provides an effective way to explore these patterns, allowing viewers to compare trends across time and identify meaningful differences between the two animation traditions.
# 4. Datasets

The project will rely on publicly available movie datasets that provide information about animated films, including release year, ratings, and popularity.
### IMDb Dataset

The IMDb public dataset contains information about films, including title type, release year, genres, ratings, and number of votes.

Relevant variables include:
- release year
- genre
- average rating
- number of votes

The dataset will be filtered to include only films categorized under **Animation**, allowing comparison between Western and Eastern productions.
### MyAnimeList Dataset

This dataset provides detailed information about anime productions, including:
- title
- release year
- rating
- number of users
- studio

It complements the IMDb dataset by providing a richer representation of Eastern animated productions, which are sometimes underrepresented in Western film databases.

### TMDB Dataset (optional integration)

The TMDB movie dataset includes additional information such as:
- popularity score
- production countries
- revenue
- release dates

These variables allow the analysis of the commercial and global distribution aspects of animated films.
# 5. Context and Vision

The visualization aims to provide an **exploratory comparison** between Western and Eastern animation industries.

The intended audience includes:
- movie enthusiasts
- students interested in film and media
- general audiences curious about animation trends

The goal is to allow users to explore how animation production and popularity evolved over time and how the two traditions differ in terms of reception and commercial performance.

The visualization will emphasize clarity and comparability between the two animation traditions.
# 6. Angles and Possible Visualizations

Several analytical perspectives will be explored in order to highlight different aspects of the animation industry.

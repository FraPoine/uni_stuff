Let A ⊆ Σ* be a recursive language. 
We say that a TM M “partially decides” A if, for every string s ∈ Σ∗, M(s) either runs forever or provides the correct answer as to s ∈ A. In other words: 
- ∀s ∈ Σ* --> M(s) = ∞ ∨ M(s) = 
	- 0 if s̸ ∈ A
	- 1 if s ∈ A. 
1) Prove that if M partially decides A, then L(M) ⊆ A (remember that L(M) is the language recognized by M). 
2) Prove that the opposite implication is not true by providing a simple counterexample (suggestion: let A = {0, 1}; what about a TM that accepts 0 and rejects every other string?) 
3) Prove that the property P = {M : M partially decides A} is not semantic.
4) Prove that, even though we cannot invoke Rice’s theorem, P is not recursive (suggestion: try to apply Rice’s theorem reduction to P, but pay attention: if a machine M never halts then L(M) = ∅, but M ∈ P).

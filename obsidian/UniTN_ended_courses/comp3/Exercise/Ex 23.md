Consider the following language in {0, 1}\*:
- K = {0^n\*1^n : n ∈ N} = {ε, 01, 0011, 000111, 00001111, 0000011111, . . . }
- i.e., all strings composed by a sequence of zeroes followed by the same number of ones
1. Write a single-tape Turing Machine with alphabet Σ = {-, 0, 1} that recognizes K.
2. Prove or disprove the decidability of each of the following properties of TMs:
	- P1 = {M : M decides K}, 
	- P2 = {M : M decides K in less than 100 steps}, 
	- P3 = {M : M decides K ∩ Σ100 (i.e., strings in K not longer that 100 symbols)}.
*Hint — For 23.1 use any notation you like, and encode acceptance and rejection as you prefer (0/1 on tape, two different halting states, etc.).*

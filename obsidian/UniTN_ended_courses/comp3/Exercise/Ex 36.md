Consider the following language of strings in {a, b}∗: 
L = {an1 ban2 ban3 b · · · bank : k ∈ N ∧ k ≥ 1 ∧ n1 > n2 > n3 > · · · > nk > 0}. 
Namely, a string is in L if and only if it is composed of non-empty sequences of a’s of decreasing length separated by single b’s, e.g.: 
- aaaaabaaaba ∈ L, 
- aaba ∈ L, 
- aaabaaa̸ ∈ L (two subsequences with the same number of a’s: n1 = n2 = 3), 
- aaabaaba ∈ L, 
- aaaaaaaaaaaaaaba ∈ L, 
- aaabbaa̸ ∈ L (two consecutive b’s), 
- aabaaa̸ ∈ L (the sequences of b’s are not of decreasing length). 
Prove that L ∈ L.

Let L ∈ P be a deterministic polynomial-time language on finite alphabet Σ, and let L′ and L′′ be defined as follows: 
- L′ = Σ∗ × L × Σ∗ = {w1w2w3 : w1, w3 ∈ Σ∗ ∧ w2 ∈ L}, the language of all strings on alphabet Σ that contain a word from L as a substring (contiguous sequence of symbols);
- L′′ = {σ1σ2σ3 . . . σn ∈ Σ∗ : ∃k, i1, i2, . . . ik (0 ≤ k ≤ n ∧ 1 ≤ i1 < i2 < · · · < ik ≤ n ∧ σi1 σi2 . . . σik ∈ L)}, the language of all strings containing a (non necessarily contiguous) subse- quence of symbols that compose a word in L. 
For instance, if “cat” ∈ L, then “location” and “catalog” belong to both L′ and L′′, while the words “decoration” and “croissant” only belong to L′′. 
1) Discuss the deterministic time complexity of L′ and L′′. 
2) What about their non-deterministic time complexity?

## 1 
Discuss the deterministic complexity of L' and L''

Let ML be a deterministic polynomial time TM that decides L 
- given a string x
	- to decide if x ∈ L' we need to iterate through all of its sub strings x' and check if x' ∈ L
So ML'(x): for all x' sub string of x, if ML(x') accepts then accept and halt Finally, reject and halt.

since the number of sub string of x is polynomial (quadratic) in |x|, the whole procedure is still polynomial, and L' ∈ P

on the other hand, a machine ML'' that decide L'' must iterate over all non contiguous sub sequences of characters of x, and there are 2^|x| of them, therefore, in the worst case we need to call ML(x') for an exponential number of sub sequences of x. Therefore, all we can say is that L'' ∈ EXP

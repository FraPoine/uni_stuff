Let M be a one-tape TM on some finite alphabet Σ, with states Q = {q1, . . . , qn} and transition function f : Σ × Q → Σ × Q × {←, →}. 

Suppose now that the tape has some “defective” cells that are marked with a new symbol, X, which is pre-written on tham and cannot be modified (i.e., if the machine reads X it can only write X). Our purpose is to crete a “robust” version M′ of M. 

Of course, the alphabet of M′ will be Σ′ = Σ ∪ {X}. 
Discuss a systematic way to define a new stateset Q′ and a new transition function f ′ : Σ′ × Q′ → Σ′ × Q′ × {←, →} that performs the same computation on the defective tape by “skipping” cells marked with the new symbol X. 
Assume that the initial input string also skips defective cells; for example, here is a sample initial configuration for input 0110:
- . . . - - X - \[0] 1 X 1 0 - X - - . . .

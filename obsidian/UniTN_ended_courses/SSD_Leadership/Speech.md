# Slide 2 Integrity
for Sinek, integrity mean do the right thing even when its difficult.
A leader must be an example for his team in order to build trust between the members, trust is the foundation of a safe, high-performance team.
Another interesting derivative of integrity is accountability, take responsibility of our error. In a strong team, people feel safe enough to admit failure without fear of punishment. This kind of psychological safety encourages honesty, learning, and continuous improvement—exactly the kind of culture great leaders strive to build.
A leader with integrity doesn’t seek to look good, they seek to do good.

**Joan of Arc is a powerful example of integrity.** 
Despite being young, outnumbered, and threatened with death, she stayed true to her beliefs and mission. Even when given the chance to lie and save herself, she chose honesty over safety. Her actions proved that real leadership is about standing firm in your values, no matter the cost.

#### Input & output
The Input / output may be classified as: 
- **Sequential**: it is received by / sent on a single modality, though that modality can change over time 
- **Simultaneous**: it is received by / sent on multiple modalities, and treated separately 
- **Composite**: it is received by / sent on multiple modalities at the same time and treated as a single, integrated “composite” input
Nigay & Coutaz (1993) classified multimodal systems depending on: 
- Fusion method (combined or independent) 
- Use of modalities (sequential or parallel)

### Signals Processing vs. Digital Signal Processing
- Signal processing is a field of electrical engineering that focuses on the analysis, manipulation, and synthesis of signals 
- Digital signal processing (DSP) is a subfield of signal processing that deals exclusively with digital signals

#### Operating on signals
- **Transforms**
	- Sometimes, it's appropriate to change the domain of observation of a signal to highlight properties that are invisible in the original domain (e.g. time)
	- The most well-known is the **Fourier Transform**: any integrable signal can be expressed as a sum of sinusoidal functions. The new domain is the frequency domain
		- img at slide 68
	- The result of applying the Fourier Transform is a complex number that can be represented as magnitude and phase: 69 
		- The magnitude represents the amplitude of each sinusoidal function 
		- The phase describes the starting position of each sinusoidal function
- **Filtering**
	- extracting desired information from a signal by removing unwanted components
	- Three kinds of filters: 
		- **Low-pass**: they allow frequencies below a specific threshold (the cutoff frequency) to pass through while attenuating, or reducing, frequencies above it. Particularly useful for removing high-frequency noise from a signal 
		- **High-pass**: They allow frequencies above the cutoff frequency to pass through while attenuating lower frequencies. For example, they are used to remove DC or low-frequency components from a signal 
		- **Band-pass**: They allow the passage of frequencies within a specific range. They are useful for isolating a frequency band of interest
	- From a technically point of view, filtering is implemented as a convolution in the original domain between the signal and the filter function
- **Convolution**
	- It's a mathematical operation that combines two functions to produce a third one, which expresses how the shape of one function is modified by the other 
	- In simpler terms, convolution is an operation that "merges" two signals, a main one and a kernel that serves to modify the first

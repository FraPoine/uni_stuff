### HCI essentials
definition
- *Human-computer interaction is a discipline concerned with the design, evaluation and implementation of interactive computing systems for human use and with the study of major phenomena surrounding them*

idea
- making the complex simple

HCI ingredients 
- human beings
- Machines
- Some task to solve to achieve a common goal

key point
- Human beings and machines interact together over time

some history

Implementing the desktop metaphor
- W.I.M.P.
	- window 
	- icon
	- menu
	- pointer
- WYSIWYG
	- what you see / what you get

*WIMPs are the perfect interfaces only for creatures with a single eye, one or more single-jointed fingers… (Buxton). But, Humans are multimodal by nature!*

post-WIMP
- containing at least one interaction technique not dependent on classical 2D widgets such as menus and icons
- Sensor modality: sensory channel through which info is perceived / sent
- Multimodal so refers to the integration of info coming from / starting from different sensory channels for extracting / conveying high level meaning
- **Multimedia vs Multimodal**: 
	- **Multimedia** systems convey signals of different kinds (e.g. a ppt presentation with audio / visual clip) 
	- **Multimodal** systems interpret signals from various sensory modalities


**Human communication definition**
- *the use of verbal and nonverbal messages to create ongoing influence*
- *a process of sorting, selecting and sending symbols in such a way to help a receiver to find in his or her own mind a meaning similar to that intended by a sender*

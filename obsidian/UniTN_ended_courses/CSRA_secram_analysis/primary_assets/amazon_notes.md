## **Summary**
#### **Airspace Design**
-	Airspace below 200 feet, or the ‘Low-Speed Localized Traffic’ area
	-	terminal non-transit operations uch as surveying, videography and inspection.
	-	operations for lesser-equipped vehicles, e.g. ones without sophisticated sense-and- avoid (SAA) technology.
-	A ‘High-Speed Transit’ space, between 200 and 400 fee
	-	 well- equipped vehicles as determined by the relevant performance standards and rules
-	Between 400 and 500 feet will serve as a permanent ‘No Fly Zone’
	-	in which sUAS operators will not be permitted to fly, except in emergencies
-	This airspace model will also encompass ‘Predefined Low Risk Locations.’
	-	Altitude and equipage restrictions in these locations will be established in advance by aviation authorities. 
		
#### **Airspace Management & Operations**
-	The current airspace management system, designed for traditional manned aircraft with one pilot per vehicle, cannot handle the expected surge in small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS) operations, especially at low altitudes. Air traffic controller workload is already a major limitation, and the high volume of future sUAS traffic will require a new approach.
-	Amazon argues that a paradigm shift is needed—moving from centralized air traffic control to a more **distributed and automated system**, where operators manage **their own fleets** using advanced communication and collision-avoidance technologies. A central authority would oversee coordination and safety, but many traditional air traffic control functions would be delegated to automated systems and operators.
-	Amazon is working with aviation authorities (like the FAA and NASA) to research this **delegated and federated model**, ensuring safe integration of large-scale commercial sUAS operations.
-	**TL;DR:** The current air traffic system can't handle future drone traffic, so Amazon proposes a more automated, decentralized approach with industry operators managing their fleets under broader regulatory oversight.

## **Assets**
#### **Primary Assets (Core Advantages & Strategic Goals)**
1. **Segmented Low-Altitude Airspace Model (Below 500 ft)**
    - Amazon advocates for **segregated airspace** to separate sUAS from traditional aviation, reducing collision risks.
    - Structured into zones:
        - **0-200 ft**: Low-speed, localized operations (inspections, videography) for basic drones.
        - **200-400 ft**: High-speed transit for advanced, well-equipped drones (like Amazon’s delivery fleet).
        - **400-500 ft**: Permanent "No Fly Zone" (buffer to manned aviation).
2. **Automated & Federated Air Traffic Management**
    - **Decentralized control**: Operators manage fleets autonomously with vehicle-to-vehicle (V2V) and vehicle-to-service (V2S) communication.
    - **"Managed by exception"**: Human intervention only in emergencies; automation handles routine traffic.
    - **Reduced reliance on traditional ATC**: Scalable for high-volume drone operations.
3. **Advanced sUAS Technology (Key Differentiators)**
    - **Sense-and-Avoid (SAA) systems**: Critical for beyond-line-of-sight (BLOS) operations.
    - **Automated navigation, collision avoidance, and deconfliction**: Enables "one operator to many vehicles" model.
    - **Highly secure, reliable communication networks**: Ensures safe coordination in congested airspace.
4. **Strategic Partnerships & Regulatory Influence**
    - Collaboration with **FAA, NASA, and other aviation authorities** to shape policies.
    - Involvement in NASA’s **Unmanned Traffic Management (UTM)** program to advance automation.

#### **Secondary Assets (Supporting Elements)**
5. **Predefined Low-Risk Zones**
    - Designated areas (e.g., model airfields) with relaxed restrictions for recreational users.
6. **Research & Industry Leadership**
    - Investment in **airspace automation** and **safety protocols**.
    - Advocacy for **performance standards** across commercial/recreational sUAS.
7. **Scalability for Commercial Fleets**
    - Focus on **large-scale drone delivery networks** (e.g., Prime Air).
    - Emphasis on **cost efficiency** via automation and reduced human oversight.
